## Summary

f_masonry is a parametric brick/course texture source for Vsynth — a single `jit.gl.pix` with a gen codebox, texture+control inlet (routepass pattern). The codebox rotates each pixel into a local frame, computes course index and brick position within each course, and applies a presence/distance function to determine brick vs joint. A skip gate controls course presence across the field. The patcher is a thin wrapper around the codebox — the work is almost entirely GLSL.

**Parameter vocabulary (masonry metaphor):**  
`courses` (band spacing), `bond` (brick frequency), `mortar` (brick-to-joint ratio), `offset` (stagger between courses), `drift` (per-brick jitter), `skip` (course presence gate), `course_seed`/`brick_seed` (hash reseeding via live.numbox).

## Architecture Decisions

### ADR 1: routepass, not route

**Context:** f_masonry is a self-generating source but requires a texture signal on inlet 0 to trigger rendering in the Vsynth context.

**Decision:** Use `routepass jit_gl_texture jit_matrix <params...>` on inlet 0. Texture passes through to pix inlet 0 as render trigger; param messages are peeled off and routed to dials. The texture itself is unused by the codebox.

**Rationale:** Confirmed empirically — `jit.gl.pix vsynth` requires an incoming texture signal to render even when self-generating. Pure `route` produces no output.

**Consequences:**
- Bypass outputs black (`vec4(0.0)`) — no source texture to fall back to
- Signal chain: `routepass outlet0 → pix in0` + `routepass param outlets → dials → prepend → pix in0`

---

### ADR 2: Euclidean rhythm as closed-form, bounded-period computation

**Context:** "Which mark am I in?" is inherently sequential — you need cumulative widths to your left. GPUs don't do sequential well.

**Decision:** Bound the rhythm period to ≤ 16 slots. Beat `i` in E(k,n) lands at `floor(i * n / k)`. For a period ≤ 16, this is an unrolled comparison — no loop, no texture lookup.

**Alternatives considered:**
- Loop over beats: Not GPU-friendly; Gen may or may not unroll; fragile
- Lookup texture: Adds texture infrastructure for a small table; overkill

**Consequences:**
- `beats` and `period` are bounded integers (1–16), enforced in patcher before codebox
- The closed-form is exact for integer E(k,n); no approximation

---

### ADR 3: Offset as deterministic per-band phase accumulation

**Context:** Offset controls the phase relationship between adjacent bands. The original grain-style approach used a pinned hash per band (random identity). But f_weave's offset concept is about *neighbor relationship*, not random identity.

**Decision:** `along_phase = (along * density_scale + band_idx * offset * period) mod period`. Each band shifts by `offset * period` relative to its neighbor. Deterministic from `band_idx` — no hash.

**Rationale:** The perceptual character (aligned vs staggered) comes from the local neighbor relationship. Global pattern repeat is not a design goal. Bands 3 and 4 do whatever the math gives them.

**Consequences:**
- No hash needed for offset phase — simpler codebox
- Swing still uses a pinned hash per hit (seeded from hit_idx + band_idx) for stable jitter

---

### ADR 4: Two functions resolved empirically in Phase 0

**Context:** The curl field function (how orientation varies spatially) and continuity gate function (how band presence varies spatially) are both specified by contract, not implementation. Their exact form depends on what feels right in motion.

**Decision:** Phase 0 is a scratchpad codebox session in `~/Vsynth/patterns/weave-scratch.maxpat`. Both functions are tried and evaluated visually before any patcher JSON is written.

**Curl field contract:** Smooth, slowly-varying, zero at `curl=0`. Candidate: `sin(norm.x * freq) * curl` for lateral bend. May need 2D variant.

**Continuity gate contract:** Smooth, slowly-varying spatial gate on band presence. At `continuity=1` always returns 1.0; at `continuity=0` sparse. Candidate: low-frequency value noise over `(px, py)` scaled by `continuity`. Pure band-level gate (entire bands present/absent) is also worth trying — different character.

**Consequences:**
- Phase 0 is the riskiest phase — unknown output
- All subsequent phases depend on Phase 0 conclusions
- Plan and tasks may need minor revision after Phase 0

### ADR 6: Three modulation inlets — slot, brick, pixel space

**Context:** Phase 5 initially implemented two inlets (u-mod, v-mod) both
sampling in brick identity space (quantized to slot/course center). This
preserved structural coherence but eliminated sub-brick and screen-space
variation. The u/v axis split collapsed into redundancy — both inlets were
doing the same operation on different axes.

**Decision:** Replace u-mod/v-mod with three inlets corresponding to three
distinct coordinate spaces:
- **Slot mod** (`in 2`) — sampled at `vec(slot/bond_scale, band_idx/course_scale)`.
  Brick identity space. One value per brick unit, quantized within brick.
- **Brick mod** (`in 3`) — sampled at `vec(along_frac, across_phase)`.
  Intra-brick space. Tiles texture per brick face. Sub-brick variation.
- **Pixel mod** (`in 4`) — sampled at `norm`. Screen space. Continuous
  across the field; brick structure articulates it into structural variation.

All three use identical target/depth model (int chooser + bipolar float).
`_eff` variables accumulate deltas from all three inlets additively.

**Rationale:** The three spaces are architecturally distinct and none is
redundant. Screen space is not equivalent to upstream texture processing —
upstream textures get chopped by the brick mask rather than modulating brick
structure parameters. Intra-brick space is uniquely capable of per-face
variation. Brick identity space gives the quantized per-unit behavior.
Collapsing u/v into a single slot mod inlet is correct because the axis
distinction was a sampling detail, not a structural difference.

**Consequences:**
- `numinlets` on pix increases from 3 to 5 (in0 routepass + 3 mod inlets)
- 4 params replaced by 6: `slot_mod_target/depth`, `brick_mod_target/depth`,
  `pixel_mod_target/depth`
- Geometry block ordering: brick mod sampling must follow full geometry block
  (needs `along_frac`, `across_phase`); slot mod needs only early geometry
  (`band_idx`, `slot`); pixel mod needs only `norm`
- Phase5c codebox is superseded; new codebox written from scratch for M002

### ADR 7: Candidate-search slot ownership (fixes drift/regularity rigidity)

**Context:** Field testing (2026-07-05) showed that at high `drift` with
`regularity=0`, the output is not the intended organic broken-course look but
a "sliced barcode" — bars of random width randomly anchored to one edge or
the other, always hard-clipped at the same fixed slot boundaries. Root cause,
confirmed by reading `codebox_phase6.gen` directly: `slot = floor(...)` is a
single lookup from the pixel's own position, and every downstream effect
(`drift_offset`, presence hash, `mark_dist`) only ever reshuffles phase
*within* that one fixed slot. The slot boundary itself never moves regardless
of parameter values — the same structural shape as `f_vf_seeds`' pre-
Evolution-2 Voronoi (single-owner grid, hard clip, selection tuning alone
can't produce migration or overlap). Confirmed empirically, not just by
analogy — this is the same lesson, independently re-derived.

**Decision:** Replace the single `slot` lookup with a 3-candidate search
(`base_slot - 1, base_slot, base_slot + 1`), winner-take-all (no multi-owner
compositing — real bricks don't overlap, unlike `f_vf_seeds`' marks). Each
candidate `s` computes its own drifted center in absolute along-space;
whichever candidate's center is nearest (after a `regularity`-driven priority
bias, see below) wins the pixel. The winner's slot identity replaces `slot`
everywhere downstream, including A-space texture-mod sampling — which means
the search must run *before* A-sampling, reversing today's order.

**Per-candidate formulas:**
```
drift_amt_s   = drift_raw_s * drift * 0.5 * (1.0 - quantize)   // quantize = search snap strength
cand_center_s = s + 0.5 + drift_amt_s
raw_dist_s    = abs(base_along - cand_center_s)                 // true distance — drives mark shape
bias_dist_s   = raw_dist_s - pri_raw_s * (1.0 - regularity) * PRIORITY_SCALE  // selection only
```
Winner = min `bias_dist_s` (pairwise `step`/`mix`, no arrays/branching per
existing codebox convention). Winner's **raw** (unbiased) distance feeds
`mark_dist = raw_dist_winner * 2.0` — bias only decides who wins, never how
big the mark renders.

**Three sub-decisions resolved this session (2026-07-05):**
- **`regularity`** repurposed from edge-hugging-within-a-fixed-slot to a
  *priority bias in the search itself* — same conceptual shape as
  `f_vf_seeds`' `field_priority`. At `regularity=1`, nearest-candidate-always-
  wins (even grid). At `regularity=0`, a candidate can win territory it isn't
  geometrically nearest to, producing genuinely uneven brick spacing as an
  emergent property of selection. **Explicitly breaks the spec's original
  "same average density" acceptance criterion at `regularity=0`** — accepted
  as a real, intentional tradeoff in exchange for a more expressive
  irregularity, not an oversight. Spec's acceptance criteria need updating
  to reflect this (see below).
- **`quantize`** repurposed from "slot-quantized vs. continuous drift" (which
  had no coherent meaning once slot identity is inherently discrete via the
  search) to the search's own **snap strength** — scales `drift_amt_s`
  directly, so `quantize=1` collapses candidates back toward the grid
  (drift has no effect on position) and `quantize=0` lets drift displace
  candidate centers freely.
- **`phase`** now shifts `base_along` *before* the search runs (rather than
  wrapping locally within a fixed slot as before), so scrolling genuinely
  reassigns brick ownership across cells as courses animate — expected to
  also fix the current treadmill-only scrolling behavior as a side effect.

**Known, accepted limitation (same lesson as `f_vf_seeds`):** winning
contested territory via the `regularity` priority bias does not make a brick
*bigger* — `mark_size` is still set by `mortar` around the true (unbiased)
center. A brick that wins extra territory at low `regularity` may render as
more surrounding mortar/gap rather than a visibly larger, relocated brick.
Selection alone doesn't grow the mark — confirmed true for seeds, assumed
true here without yet re-verifying empirically. If the flat-mortar look is a
problem once seen in Max, the fix is a `mag_weight`-equivalent (mark size
scales with territory won) — deliberately deferred, do not add preemptively.

**Deferred, not decided:** structural A-space modulation
(`drift_mod_amt_a`, `regularity_mod_amt_a`, `quantize_mod_amt_a` targeting
the candidate search itself) would require the search's own inputs to depend
on A-sampling, which depends on the search's winner — circular. Since
nothing is currently wired to these targets (spec's own target-list section
already flags this as unconfirmed/hypothetical), this dependency is left
unresolved rather than forced. Appearance-param A/B/C modulation
(`mortar`, `softness`, `width`, `roundness`, `course_color`, `brick_color`)
is untouched and still applies after the search resolves, exactly as
spec'd.

**Consequences:**
- Geometry block reorders: base geometry → candidate search (drift, priority
  bias, winner select) → A-sampling keyed to winner's `brick_uv` → rest of
  pipeline unchanged, fed by winner's `raw_dist`/slot identity
- `mark_slot` for color hashing becomes the winner's slot identity directly —
  the current `mark_center`/`floor()` recovery dance is no longer needed
- Spec's acceptance criteria for `regularity` need rewriting (see spec.md)
- Existing `slot_hash`-based edge-hugging behavior (`mark_dist_rnd`) is
  removed entirely, replaced by the priority-bias mechanism above
- Scratch-test in Max before touching `codebox_phase6.gen` in production,
  per standing practice — this is a substantial geometry reorder, not a
  parameter tweak

**Addendum (2026-07-05) — scratch-tested in Max, `quantize` removed:**

E001a–E001i all confirmed in Max: regression check passes at
`drift=0, regularity=1, quantize=1, phase=0` (matches old shipped
behavior); `drift` alone produces real cross-boundary migration, not a
sliced barcode; `regularity=0` produces genuinely uneven, merged-looking
brick widths as contested territory is won — confirmed to read as "a
wider brick," not a normal brick with extra surrounding mortar, so the
`mag_weight`-equivalent flagged as a possible follow-up is **not
needed**; 60+fps; no flicker/seams at candidate boundaries.

**`quantize` removed entirely.** Matt caught, and the math confirms: as
implemented, `drift_scale = drift * 0.5 * (1.0 - quantize)` is a pure
linear scalar on `drift` — any `(drift, quantize)` pair is reproducible
by `drift` alone at `quantize=0`. Not a new axis of control, a redundant
second fader on the same one. A genuinely orthogonal version (stepped/
discrete positional snapping, changing the *character* of displacement
rather than its magnitude) was offered but explicitly declined — not
something Matt asked for or wants, just a way to save the param name.
`codebox_phase8.gen` drops `quantize` entirely: `drift_scale = drift *
0.5`, no `Param quantize` declaration. Spec's param table updated to
remove the row. Production promotion (E001j onward) will also need
`quantize`'s `live.dial`/route entry/`definition.py` field removed from
the patcher — not done yet, this addendum covers the scratch codebox
only.
